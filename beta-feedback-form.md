# ProcrastiNation — Beta Feedback Form

> Copy these questions into a Google Form, Typeform, or Notion form.
> Share with beta users after they've had at least 2–3 days with the app.
> Target: 10 responses minimum before Phase 2 GTM.

---

## Suggested Form Header

**Title:** Help us build something you'll actually use.

**Description:**
> You're one of the first people to try ProcrastiNation. That makes your opinion genuinely valuable — not in a corporate way, in a "this will literally shape what we build next" way. 7 questions, takes 3 minutes. Be brutally honest.

---

## The Questions

---

### Q1 — Friction Finder
**"What almost stopped you from signing up or using the app for the first time?"**

*Type: Paragraph (open text)*

*Why we ask: Identifies the biggest drop-off points in onboarding. Even "nothing" is useful data.*

---

### Q2 — Core Loop Validator
**"Did the AI plan actually help you make progress on your task? What happened?"**

*Type: Paragraph (open text)*

*Why we ask: This is the single most important question. We need to know if the core product works, in their own words.*

---

### Q3 — Friction in the Flow
**"Was there any moment where you got confused, frustrated, or just stopped using the app?"**

*Type: Paragraph (open text)*

*Why we ask: Surfaces UX bugs and confusing flows we can't see from the outside.*

---

### Q4 — The "Tell a Friend" Test
**"On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to recommend ProcrastiNation to a friend who struggles with procrastination?"**

*Type: Linear scale (1 = not at all, 10 = already texted them)*

**Follow-up (show regardless of score):**
**"What's the main reason for that score?"**

*Type: Paragraph (open text)*

*Why we ask: This is NPS. The follow-up is where the gold is — it tells us exactly what drives or kills word-of-mouth.*

---

### Q5 — Willingness to Pay Signal
**"If ProcrastiNation added [feature], would that make it worth paying $12/month for?"**

*Type: Multiple choice — pick up to 2*

Options:
- [ ] Daily nudges reminding me of unfinished tasks
- [ ] A weekly summary of what I accomplished and what's next
- [ ] Task templates for common situations (studying, job hunting, projects)
- [ ] Private co-working sessions with friends
- [ ] The ability to "freeze" my streak so I don't lose it
- [ ] Honestly, I'd pay for what's already here
- [ ] I wouldn't pay for any version of this

*Why we ask: Directly prioritizes our Pro feature roadmap. Avoids building things nobody wants.*

---

### Q6 — Who Are You? (ICP Validator)
**"Which of these best describes you?"**

*Type: Multiple choice — pick one*

Options:
- College or grad student
- Early-career professional (0–5 years in)
- Mid-career professional
- Freelancer or entrepreneur
- Someone diagnosed with ADHD or similar
- Just a chronic procrastinator trying to get it together
- Other

*Why we ask: Validates our ICP assumptions. If answers skew somewhere unexpected, we adjust targeting.*

---

### Q7 — The Open Floor
**"Anything else you want us to know? Feature ideas, complaints, things you loved — all welcome."**

*Type: Paragraph (open text)*

*Why we ask: Some of the best product insights come from the things people say when they're not being asked a specific question.*

## What to Do With Results

**After 5 responses:**
- Flag any Q3 friction mentioned by 2+ people → fix it immediately
- Note the most-selected Q5 feature → validate against roadmap priority

**After 10 responses:**
- Calculate average NPS (Q4 scores)
- Identify the dominant Q6 user type → sharpen ICP
- Pull the 2–3 most compelling Q2 quotes → use as testimonials on landing page
- Review Q7 open text for unexpected insights

**Decision gate:**
- Average NPS ≥ 7 AND at least one "it actually helped me finish" in Q2 → proceed to Phase 2 GTM
- Average NPS < 6 OR no Q2 core loop validation → fix product first, re-survey
